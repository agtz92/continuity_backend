Continuity — the project manager that makes you finish

Built for people who start more than they finish. Every project you begin must reach a decision — finished, launched, paused on purpose, or deliberately killed. Never abandoned.

How to read this. It's organized in layers. The first two sections give a business reader the full value in about two minutes. Each section after adds depth, ending with a clearly separated Technical Annex. Stop at any layer and you'll still have a complete, accurate picture.

1Executive summary

Continuity is a personal productivity workspace with one strong opinion: every project you start must reach a decision.

Most tools are great at helping you add things — more tasks, more notes, more lists. They stay silent about the real problem of ambitious people: a growing graveyard of half-finished projects that quietly drains time, momentum, and confidence. Continuity is the first tool built to close that loop. A project here is never allowed to simply drift. It must end up Finished, Launched, Paused on purpose, or deliberately Killed — never abandoned.

What it isA workspace for projects, tasks, ideas, routines and notes — on web and mobile — with a built-in AI assistant.
The problemThe hidden cost of unfinished work: stalled side-projects, forgotten commitments, and eroding trust in your own ideas.
Who it's forFounders, freelancers and side-project builders — people who "start more than they finish."
Why it's differentIt introduces forced closure: a project that goes quiet is surfaced and you're asked to decide its fate. It even keeps a "graveyard" and uses AI to show you why you keep abandoning things.
How you reach itA web app, a mobile app (iOS, in TestFlight), and — uniquely — from inside Claude (Anthropic's AI) through a secure connector.
Business modelFreemium: a free tier plus two paid tiers (Pro, Studio). Currently in private beta (50 spots).
The one-line pitch: Continuity turns "I'll get to it later" into a decision.

2What you can do with Continuity

Everything below is a capability the product has today — described as a benefit, not a mechanism.

Manage projects that can't quietly die

  • Capture a project with its purpose, the why, and the next concrete step.
  • Move it through a clear lifecycle and never lose track of where it stands.
  • When a project goes quiet it's flagged Stalled, and you're asked: ship it, kill it, or get help.
  • Close with intention — pausing captures context and the next action; killing captures the reason and lessons learned.
  • Revive a paused or killed project later — the AI reads your old closure notes to brief you on where you left off.
Idea→ Active→ Stalled→ Paused→ Launched→ Killed→ Archived

Break work down & do it

Tasks with due dates, times, effort estimates and blockers — plus a focused "Today" view of what's due, overdue, and already done.

Capture ideas freely

Jot ideas with no project required, then promote the promising ones into full projects in one step.

Build consistency

Recurring routines (daily/weekly/monthly/custom) with cadence tracking over time.

Keep your thinking in one place

Notion-style Quick Notes with collapsible, reorderable sections — plus notes attached to specific projects.

See your real productivity

An analytics dashboard: cadence, status breakdown, backlog trend, top projects, the idea→project→task funnel, and more.

Plan visually

A Day / Week / Month calendar laying out your projects and routines so you can see your real load.

Get AI help that knows your work

"Loop" answers questions about your projects and, on paid plans, creates and updates tasks through conversation.

Stay reminded

Connect notification channels (Telegram today; more planned) to get nudges about what's pending.

The central objects you work with: Projects (the heart of it), Tasks, Ideas, Routines, and Notes — tied together by an Activity log that records what you accomplish, and a Graveyard that remembers what you let go.

4What makes Continuity distinctive

The handful of things that genuinely set Continuity apart. For each: why it matters to the business first, then the technical "why" for those who want it.

4.1 · Forced closure — the core idea

Why it matters. Every other tool lets work pile up silently. Continuity's reason to exist is that it refuses to let a project quietly rot. After a stretch of inactivity, a project is automatically marked Stalled and the user faces a simple, humane choice: finish it, kill it, or ask for help. This is the feature the whole brand is built on, and the reason to choose it over a generic to-do app.

The detail. A project's life is an explicit state machine (idea→active→stalled→paused→launched→killed→archived). A scheduled background job sweeps active projects and stalls the quiet ones — and treats that automatic transition as a non-event, so the system never fools itself into thinking the user was active.

4.2 · The Graveyard and AI "autopsy"

Why it matters. Killing a project is reframed from failure into a learning moment. Killed projects don't vanish — they go to a Graveyard, each carrying why it died and what was learned. After a few kills, the AI looks across all of them and surfaces the pattern: why this person keeps abandoning work. No checklist app can offer that.

The detail. Killing captures structured closure notes (reason, learnings, would-restart). A best-effort AI reflection runs per kill; once there are three or more, a second AI pass computes a cross-project insight, cached as a single record and refreshable on demand.

4.3 · One AI, three front doors — including inside Claude

Why it matters. "Loop" doesn't only live in the app. The same intelligence is available on the website, in the mobile app, and directly inside Claude via an industry-standard connector — so a power user can manage projects just by talking to the AI they already use daily. The commercial angle: through that connector, AI usage is billed to the user's own Claude account, not to Continuity — making this distribution channel nearly free to run.

The detail. Web assistant, mobile assistant and external connector all call the same underlying tool layer. The connector is an MCP (Model Context Protocol) server — Anthropic's open standard for tool integrations — secured by a full OAuth 2.1 flow, with each connection auditable and revocable. The server is built, deployed and tested today; its public settings toggle is held behind a "Coming soon" label pending launch (see Section 5).

4.4 · Privacy by design in analytics

Why it matters. Continuity measures engagement to run the business, but without storing the content of what you do. For a product holding your most personal ambitions, "we count that you were active, we don't read what you wrote" is a real trust signal.

The detail. Interaction metrics are daily counts per user and per channel (web / mobile / connector). No message content is persisted in those metrics, and a recording failure never breaks the user's request.

4.5 · A marketing site engineered to be fast

Why it matters. First impressions convert; a slow landing page costs sign-ups. The public site was rebuilt to load fast even on a phone on a weak connection.

The detail. Marketing pages are pre-built static pages cached at the edge, with the heavy app code, AI client and animation libraries kept out of the public bundle — roughly a 44% cut in initial page weight, and images shrunk from megabytes to tens of kilobytes.

4.6 · Fair, transparent limits per plan

Why it matters. The free-to-paid model is enforced cleanly and predictably, so users always know what they get and upgrades feel fair.

The detail. Every limit (projects, tasks, ideas, routines, notes, plus AI message/token allowances) is defined once per plan and enforced consistently — whether the user creates by hand or asks the AI. Exact numbers in the Annex.

ESecurity & privacy posture

  • Authentication on every request via Supabase JWT; identity derived server-side, never trusted from the client.
  • The admin surface is gated by an explicit flag and every admin action is audit-logged.
  • The external connector uses an OAuth flow; connections are recorded (authorized / refreshed / revoked) and revocable by user or admin.
  • Analytics store counts, not content — no message bodies persisted in interaction metrics; metric failures never break the request.
  • The bug-report channel is intentionally one-way (user → admin inbox), with a soft per-user rate limit.

FNotable engineering decisions

  • Static-first marketing site. Public pages pre-rendered and edge-cached; locale in the URL (not cookies) so pages stay static; heavy libraries excluded from the public bundle. ~44% smaller first-load JavaScript.
  • Idempotent, config-driven operations. Background jobs (welcome emails, inactivity reclaim, stall sweeps) are idempotent and tunable via a runtime config table without redeploy; emails default to a safe dry_run mode.
  • Shared tool layer for all AI surfaces keeps the in-app assistant and the external connector behaviorally identical, gated by the same plan checks.
  • Active modularization. Oversized files are tracked in AUDITORIA_CODIGO.md and being decomposed — internal code-health work, not user-facing.

GInternationalization

Full English / Spanish support across web and mobile (next-intl on web, i18next-ICU on mobile). Marketing locale is encoded in the URL (/ for English, /es for Spanish) to keep pages statically cacheable.

HTesting & deployment

  • Backend: pytest + pytest-django (in-memory SQLite, Supabase JWT stubbed). Deployed on Render with scheduled cron jobs for lifecycle tasks.
  • Web: Vitest + Testing Library with a mocked GraphQL client; deployed on Vercel.
  • Mobile: built and distributed through EAS; first build in Apple TestFlight.

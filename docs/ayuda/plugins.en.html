Plugins

Plugins connect Continuity with the tools you already use, so your work doesn't live on separate islands. From Settings → Plugins you see every available plugin in a single list, each with its description and connection status. This guide walks the overview: how to get there, what the list shows, and how to read each card. (The step-by-step for each connection lives inside its own card.)

How to use it

  • Open Settings from your account menu and pick Plugins in the side menu.
  • Or go straight to the /settings/plugins route.
  • The side menu is sticky: jump between Profile, Notifications, Appearance, Billing and Plugins without losing context.
  • On mobile the menu sits at the top, stacked; on desktop it's on the left.

What each one does

  • Google Tasks — brings your existing Google Tasks into Continuity.
  • Claude — lets you check and manage your projects, tasks and ideas right from a conversation with Claude.
  • The list will grow: integrations like GitHub, Linear, Notion and Slack will live here soon.

How to use it

  • Tap a card to open that plugin's detail.
  • The status on the right tells you at a glance whether it's already connected.
  • You don't need to connect anything to browse: the list is always available.
This guide covers the overview only. Each plugin's detail (connect button, account authorization, and options) lives inside its own card and is documented separately.

▲Connected shows in green (accent); Not connected in neutral gray.

How to read the status

  • Connected = the account is already linked and the plugin is working for you.
  • Not connected = not linked yet; open its detail to connect it.
  • The status is computed automatically from your account; there's nothing to edit in the list.
  • A plugin can show Not connected even though it's available: it just means you haven't enabled it yet.

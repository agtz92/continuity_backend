# Plugins

**Plugins** connect Continuity with the tools you already use, so your work doesn't live on separate islands. From **Settings → Plugins** you see every available plugin in a single list, each with its description and connection status. This guide walks the *overview*: how to get there, what the list shows, and how to read each card.

---

## 1. Getting to Plugins

[image: plugins-01-settingsnav.png]

Plugins live in **Settings**, in the left side menu. The active **Plugins** row (plug icon 🔌) is highlighted.

**How to use it**
- Open **Settings** from your account menu and pick **Plugins** in the side menu.
- Or go straight to the `/settings/plugins` route.
- The side menu is sticky: jump between Profile, Notifications, Appearance, Billing and Plugins without losing context.
- On mobile the menu sits at the top, stacked; on desktop it's on the left.

---

## 2. The plugins list

[image: plugins-02-overview.png]

One card per available plugin, with its status in plain sight. Today there are two: **Google Tasks** and **Claude**. Each row is clickable and opens its detail screen.

**What each one does**
- **Google Tasks** — brings your existing Google Tasks into Continuity.
- **Claude** — lets you check and manage your projects, tasks and ideas right from a conversation with Claude.
- The list will grow: integrations like GitHub, Linear, Notion and Slack will live here soon.

**How to use it**
- **Tap a card** to open that plugin's detail.
- The status on the right tells you at a glance whether it's already connected.
- You don't need to connect anything to browse: the list is always available.

> **Note:** this guide covers the **overview** only. Each plugin's detail (connect button, account authorization, and options) lives inside its own card and is documented separately.

---

## 3. Anatomy of a card

[image: plugins-03-plugincard.png]

Each row has four parts:

1. **Icon** — the tool's logo/symbol.
2. **Name & description** — what it is and what it's for, in one line.
3. **Status** — *Connected* or *Not connected*.
4. **Chevron ›** — signals the row opens a detail screen.

The whole row is the button: tapping any part (icon, text or status) opens the detail.

[image: plugins-04-plugincard.png]

**How to read the status**
- **Connected** (green/accent) = the account is already linked and the plugin is working for you.
- **Not connected** (neutral gray) = not linked yet; open its detail to connect it.
- The status is computed automatically from your account; there's nothing to edit in the list.
- A plugin can show **Not connected** even though it's available: it just means you haven't enabled it yet.

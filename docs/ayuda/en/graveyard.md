# Project Graveyard

The **Graveyard** tab (the tombstone icon) holds the projects you *killed with intention*. It isn't a trash bin: it's a library of what didn't work, with your closure notes, what you learned, and whether you'd restart it. Loop can read them all and find the pattern. And if you change your mind, any project can be **revived**.

---

## 1. Overview

[image: graveyard-01-overview.png]

A list of the projects you killed, newest death first. Top to bottom:

1. **Header** — tombstone icon 🪦, title, and the subtitle “a library of what didn't work”.
2. **Metric** — *“Would restart N of M”*: how many of your dead projects you said you'd start again.
3. **Autopsy** — the purple block with the pattern Loop spots across your deaths.
4. **Tombstones** — one card per dead project, sorted by date of death (most recent on top).

> **Note:** only projects you kill **with intention** land here (from the project → “Kill with intention”). A **stalled** project doesn't fall in on its own: the app nudges you to decide (keep, pause, or kill), and only choosing “kill it” moves it to the graveyard with its notes.

---

## 2. Autopsy (Loop)

[image: graveyard-02-autopsy.png]

The pattern repeating across your dead projects, written by Loop. It appears once you have **3+ dead projects**; Loop computes the pattern and caches it.

**How to use it**
- It's **read-only**: it sums up the common “why” behind your deaths.
- **“Ask Loop to go deeper”** opens the assistant with a more detailed breakdown.
- With fewer than 3 dead projects you'll see a line saying there **isn't enough to go on** yet.

**How it refreshes**
- The pattern is **recomputed** the next time you kill a project.
- If it's out of date, you'll see it flagged as such under the text.

---

## 3. Anatomy of a tombstone

[image: graveyard-03-card.png]

Each dead project is a card with a red left border. Under the name you'll see **“Lived N days”** and the **date of death**.

**How to use it**
- **Lived N days** = days from when you created the project until today.
- **Killed …** = the date you closed it with intention.
- The **Revive** button (top-right) opens the flow to bring it back (section 5).
- Below it are the **closure notes** you wrote when you killed it (section 4).

**How to edit it**
- The graveyard is **read-only**: notes aren't edited here.
- Cards sort themselves by **date of death**, most recent first.
- To “pull” a project back out of here, use **Revive**.

---

## 4. Closure notes

[image: graveyard-04-closurenotes.png]

What you wrote in the closing ritual when you killed the project: three text fields (**Why it was killed**, **What it taught**, **Would restart**) and an optional **AI reflection**.

**How to use it**
- **Why it was killed** and **What it taught** are the two notes you'll always see.
- **Would restart** (with the ↻ icon) shows only if you answered it; it feeds the metric up top.
- The **AI reflection** (purple ✦ block) shows if Loop left a note on that project.

**How they're filled**
- These notes are written **once**, in the project's **“Kill with intention”** dialog.
- Here in the graveyard they're **read-only**: they're a record, not a form.

> The same notes block is reused for **paused** projects (with “Where you stopped” and your next action) on the “Welcome back” card. In the graveyard you'll always see the **killed** variant.

---

## 5. Reviving a project

[image: graveyard-05-revive.png]

Bring a dead project back, as an **Active** project or as an **Idea** to re-validate. It opens from the tombstone's **Revive** button and reminds you what you said you'd change.

**How to use it**
- **Active** ⚡ returns it to your in-progress projects right away.
- **Idea (re-validate)** 💡 sends it to Ideas to test again before committing.
- **Leave it dead** closes the dialog with no changes.

**Mind the cap**
- A dead project **doesn't count** toward your plan; reviving it as **Active** makes it count again.
- If you're already at your active-project cap, the app warns you instead of reviving it.
- The dialog shows your current usage (e.g. **3 / 5 active**) before you decide.

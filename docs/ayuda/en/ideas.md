# Ideas Parking Lot

The **Ideas** tab is your *parking lot*: park new ideas here so you don't abandon what you're already doing, and promote them to a project when you're ready. It's the space between “having an idea” and “starting a project.” This view's accent color is **purple**.

---

## 1. Overview

[image: ideas-01-overview.png]

The header with the “Ideas Parking Lot” title, the search box, the **Capture** button (on desktop), and the grid of idea cards. Top to bottom:

1. **Header** — title, search box, and the *Capture* button.
2. **Subtitle** — reminds you what it's for: park without abandoning what you're on now.
3. **Card grid** — each idea with its lightbulb, title, reason, and notes.
4. **Create button (FAB)** — floating purple button bottom-right (on mobile), a shortcut to capture.

> **Note:** the parking lot has no dates or statuses: an idea just waits. If one sits undecided for too long, the **Today** view flags it under **“Stale ideas”** (30+ days) so you promote it or let it go.

---

## 2. Anatomy of an idea

[image: ideas-02-card.png]

Each card shows, top to bottom: **lightbulb + title**, the **reason** in italics after an arrow (→), and the **notes**.

**How to use it**
- The purple lightbulb 💡 and the **title** are the only required part.
- The **reason** is your “why it matters,” if you wrote one.
- Free-form **notes** sit below, also optional.

**How to edit it**
- **Promote to project** turns the idea into a brand-new project.
- **Edit** opens the idea to change title, reason, or notes.
- **Delete** drops it to make room for something new.

> The **reason** and **notes** are hidden when empty: a card can be just a lightbulb and a title. Still, writing the “why” helps a lot when you return to the idea weeks later.

---

## 3. Capture an idea

[image: ideas-03-create.png]

Park it in seconds without losing the thread of what you were doing. **To open the capture form:** tap **+ Capture** (desktop) or the purple FAB bottom-right (mobile). Only the **Idea** field (marked \*) is required.

**How to use it**
- Type a short **title**: that's enough to park the idea.
- Optional: the **“why does this matter?”** and some **notes** for your future self.
- **Capture** saves it and adds it to the grid instantly.

**How to edit it**
- The same modal is reused to **edit** (titled “Edit Idea”).
- With no title, the **Capture** button stays disabled.
- **Cancel** or the ✕ closes without saving.

---

## 4. Promote to project

[image: ideas-04-promote.png]

When an idea is ready to start, turn it into a project with one tap. The idea leaves the parking lot and shows up as an **active project** in the Projects tab.

**How to use it**
- Tap **Promote to project** on the card.
- A **project** is created with the idea's title and reason as a starting point.
- The idea **leaves Ideas**: it's no longer a “maybe,” it's a project.

**How to edit it**
- From the project, add tasks, next steps, and hours.
- Changed your mind? Manage the project from the **Projects** tab.

> Promoting is one-way: the idea becomes a project. If you only meant to save it, use **Edit** instead of promoting.

---

## 5. Search your ideas

[image: ideas-05-filters.png]

The search box filters live by **title**, **reason**, and **notes** as you type. If nothing matches you'll see **“No ideas match …”** with your term in quotes.

**How to use it**
- Type in the **search box**: it filters live, no search button.
- It matches each idea's title, reason, and notes.
- Clear the text to see all your ideas again.

**How to edit it**
- There are no categories or tags: search is the only filter.
- The app decides the order; you decide what to promote or drop.

---

## 6. Empty state

[image: ideas-06-empty.png]

What you see before you park your first idea: a single **“Capture first idea”** button that opens the same modal from step 3.

**How to use it**
- Shows when you have **no ideas** saved yet.
- The center button opens the capture form to get started.
- As soon as you capture one, the empty state gives way to the grid.

> Tip: use the parking lot as a brain dump. Better to park a half-baked idea than lose it — you can always **Delete** the ones that don't pan out.

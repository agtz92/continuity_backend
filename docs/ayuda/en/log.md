# Activity Log

The **Activity Log** tab is the timeline of everything that happens in your workspace: tasks you complete, projects you create or move, ideas you promote, notes you write, and things you delete. Each entry is recorded with its date and, where it applies, the project it belongs to.

---

## 1. Timeline anatomy

[image: log-01-overview.png]

All your activity, newest first, grouped by date. Top to bottom:

1. **Header** — the “Activity Log” title and a search box to filter by text or project.
2. **Filters** — chips to narrow by type: All, Achievements, Notes, Changes, Deleted.
3. **Date blocks** — Today, Yesterday, This week, This month, Older; each with a count.
4. **Entries** — one row per event, with an icon, date, project (if any) and description.

> **Note:** order is always **reverse chronological** (newest on top). A date block **only appears if it has entries** after the filter and search are applied.

---

## 2. Inside an entry

[image: log-02-entry.png]

Each row sums up one event. The icon and color tell you its type at a glance.

**How to use it**
- **Icon** — color and shape by event type (✓ achievement, 📄 note, ✨ creation, ↻ status change, 🗑 deleted).
- **Date** — the day it happened (short format, in your language).
- **Project** — in green, if the entry belongs to a project.
- **Description** — a ready-made line: “Completed task…”, “Moved …: Active → Launched”, etc.

**How to edit it**
- Only **Note** entries can be edited or deleted: hover (or look on mobile) to reveal the pencil ✎ and the ✕.
- The rest are **automatic system records** and aren't editable: they reflect what you already did.
- Deleting a note asks to confirm (“Delete this log entry?”).

---

## 3. Filters & search

[image: log-03-filters.png]

Narrow the timeline by event type or by text. In the example: filter **Achievements** + search “landing”.

**How to use it**
- **All** — shows every activity, unfiltered.
- **Achievements** — completed tasks and routines, created projects, and promoted ideas.
- **Notes** — only your hand-written updates (note entries).
- **Changes** — status changes and due-date changes on projects/tasks.
- **Deleted** — projects, tasks, ideas, routines and notes you removed.
- The **search box** filters by entry text and by **project name**; it combines with the active filter.

**How to edit it**
- The filter is one chip at a time; tap another to switch.
- Clear the search text to see everything again within the filter.

---

## 4. How entries are created

[image: log-04-addupdate.png]

Most are generated for you; **notes** are written by you from a project. A note is tied to the project and shows up in the Log under the **Notes** filter.

**How to use it**
- **Automatic** — when you complete a task or routine, create/delete something, move a project's status, or change a date, Continuity writes the entry for you.
- **Notes (manual)** — open a project and use **Log update** to leave a free-form note with your progress.
- You can also **edit** notes later from the Log itself (pencil ✎).

**How to edit it**
- Logging updates keeps your projects “with momentum” and clears them from the stalled alert on Today.
- The Log is your history: handy to pick up context after a pause.

---

## 5. Empty states

[image: log-05-empty.png]

When there's no activity yet you'll see: **“No activity logged yet. Log updates from your projects to track momentum.”** (new user or freshly created workspace).

[image: log-06-empty.png]

If there's data but the filter or search returns nothing, the message changes.

**How to use it**
- The **“No activity…”** message shows only when there are no entries at all.
- With data but no matches, the text changes depending on whether there's a search (**“No log entries match…”**) or just the filter (**“No items in this filter.”**).

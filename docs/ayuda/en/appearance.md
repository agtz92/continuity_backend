# Appearance & language

From **Settings → Appearance & language** you decide how Continuity looks and reads: the *language* of the interface, the *theme* (light, dark or your system's), and the *color palette* of accent colors. The nice part: every change applies instantly and is saved to your account, so it syncs with the mobile app.

---

## 1. Reaching Appearance & language

[image: appearance-01-settingsnav.png]

The screen lives inside **Settings**, in the left-hand rail. The **Appearance & language** item carries the palette 🎨 icon; once you open it, it's the active item. All three controls —Language, Theme and Palette— appear together in a single card, in that order.

**How to use it**
- Open your account menu and go to **Settings**; in the rail choose **Appearance & language** (🎨 icon).
- You'll find the three controls in one card: Language, Theme and Color palette.
- **Back to dashboard** (top-left) leaves Settings.

**How to edit it**
- There's no “Save” button: every selection applies **instantly**.
- Each change shows a **Saved** toast and is stored on your account.
- Because it's saved to your account, it **syncs with the mobile app**.

---

## 2. Choosing a theme

[image: appearance-02-theme.png]

The theme decides the light or dark appearance of the whole app. The active theme stays highlighted inside the selector. There are four options: **Continuuit**, **Light**, **Dark** and **System**.

**How to use it**
- **Continuuit** is the brand theme and the default.
- **Light** / **Dark** pin the appearance regardless of the time.
- **System** follows your device's light/dark setting and switches on its own.

**How to edit it**
- **Tap an option** and the theme changes immediately across the app.
- It's saved to your account (**Saved** toast) and syncs with the mobile app.
- The theme controls light/dark; accent colors are set separately under **Palette**.

> **Note:** theme and palette work together. The **theme** decides the light or dark background and the **palette** decides the accent colors on top of it. That's why switching themes updates the palette swatches to preview what you'll actually see.

---

## 3. Choosing a color palette

[image: appearance-03-palette.png]

The palette changes **only the accent colors** (buttons, links, progress bars, highlights); the background and text are controlled by the Theme. Each button shows **two circles** with that palette's accent and secondary color. The active palette (**Default**) is highlighted.

**How to use it**
- The palette changes the **accents**: buttons, links, progress bars and highlights.
- It doesn't touch the background or text: that's the **Theme**'s job.
- The two circles preview the **accent** and **secondary** color.

**How to edit it**
- **Tap a palette** and the accents change instantly across the app.
- It's saved to your account (**Saved** toast) and syncs with the mobile app.
- The swatches **recompute** for the active theme so they reflect the real color.

> **Note:** there are **13 palettes** in total (Default, Continuuit, Pink, Business, Neon, Green, Turquoise, Cute, Midnight, Boho, Complimentary, Sunset, Retro). Scroll the row to see them all.

---

## 4. Choosing a language

[image: appearance-04-language.png]

The language changes the whole app interface. The active language (**English**) is highlighted. The two options are **English** and **Español**.

**How to use it**
- Changes the language of the **entire app interface**.
- It also affects the **digests and notifications** you receive by email.

**How to edit it**
- **Tap** English or Español; the app re-renders immediately in that language.
- It's saved to your account (**Saved** toast) and syncs with the mobile app.

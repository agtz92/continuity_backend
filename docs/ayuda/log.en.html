Activity Log

The Activity Log tab is the timeline of everything that happens in your workspace: tasks you complete, projects you create or move, ideas you promote, notes you write, and things you delete. Each entry is recorded with its date and, where it applies, the project it belongs to. This guide walks the view top to bottom: the date-grouped timeline, the anatomy of an entry, the filters and search, and how new entries get created.

Parts of a row

  • Icon — color and shape by event type (✓ achievement, 📄 note, ✨ creation, ↻ status change, 🗑 deleted).
  • Date — the day it happened (short format, in your language).
  • Project — in green, if the entry belongs to a project.
  • Description — a ready-made line: “Completed task…”, “Moved …: Active → Launched”, etc.

How to edit it

  • Only Note entries can be edited or deleted: hover (or look on mobile) to reveal the pencil ✎ and the ✕.
  • The rest are automatic system records and aren't editable: they reflect what you already did.
  • Deleting a note asks to confirm (“Delete this log entry?”).

How to use it

  • All — shows every activity, unfiltered.
  • Achievements — completed tasks and routines, created projects, and promoted ideas.
  • Notes — only your hand-written updates (note entries).
  • Changes — status changes and due-date changes on projects/tasks.
  • Deleted — projects, tasks, ideas, routines and notes you removed.
  • The search box filters by entry text and by project name; it combines with the active filter.

How to edit it

  • The filter is one chip at a time; tap another to switch.
  • Clear the search text to see everything again within the filter.

How entries are created

Most are generated for you; notes are written by you from a project.

▲A note is tied to the project and shows up in the Log under the Notes filter.

Where entries come from

  • Automatic — when you complete a task or routine, create/delete something, move a project's status, or change a date, Continuity writes the entry for you.
  • Notes (manual) — open a project and use Log update to leave a free-form note with your progress.
  • You can also edit notes later from the Log itself (pencil ✎).

Why it matters

  • Logging updates keeps your projects “with momentum” and clears them from the stalled alert on Today.
  • The Log is your history: handy to pick up context after a pause.
